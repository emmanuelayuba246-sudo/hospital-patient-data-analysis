# Hospital-Patient-Data-Analysis

## Project Preview
This analysis was conducted to identify common medical conditions, understand patient demographics, and uncover healthcare trends that can support better decision-making, resource allocation, and improved patient care. 

This project analyzed an exclusive dataset containing 40,235 patients, 40,341 doctors, 39,876 hospitals, 55,500 admissions, patient demographics, medical conditions, billing, and test results.  

## Problem Statement 
Despite maintaining digital health records, hospitals face challenges in:

- Identifying common medical conditions and their demographic patterns
- Understanding how patient age, gender, and admission type affect billing and hospital usage
- Detecting which hospitals and doctors manage the highest patient loads
- Assessing how insurance coverage impacts billing and hospital stay trends. 

## Objectives 

This analysis aims to: 
- Identify the most frequent medical conditions and their distribution by age and gender
- Evaluate hospital and doctor performance based on patient volume and billing amounts
- Analyze patient admission patterns (urgent, elective, emergency)
- Explore relationships between demographics, test results, and medical conditions
- Provide actionable insights to improve hospital management and patient services.

## Tools Used 
- Power BI
- Microsoft Excel 

# Key Findings and Observations 

## Observation 1

The top common medical conditions among patients were;
- Arthritis (8019 patient)
- Diabetes (7950 patient)
- Hypertension (7870 patient)
- Cancer (7853 patient)
- Obesity (7844 patient)
- Asthma (7837 patient) 

This shows that the dataset has relatively close amount of patient counts, indicating that patient distribution across these diseases is balanced, suggesting that healthcare demand is spread across multiple medical conditions rather than concentrated in one area. The close frequency among the leading diseases shows a diverse disease burden within the patient population. 
Since the top diseases occur at close rates, healthcare resources may need to be distributed evenly across multiple treatment areas. 


## Observation 2 

The data analyzed a high number of patients between age 18 to 85 were affected by Arthritis, Asthma, Cancer, Diabetes, Hypertension, and Obesity. Indicating a higher concentration of medical cases within the adult population. 

The gender distribution of affected patients was nearly balanced, with 27,726 female patients and 27774 male patients recorded across age 1 to 89. 

The slight difference between male and female patient counts suggests that the diseases impacted both genders at almost equal rates. 


## Observation 3

Micheal Smith had the highest frequency of patient records, appearing 27 times in the dataset.

## Observation 5 

The distribution of patients across major insurance providers was relatively balanced within the dataset
- Cigna recorded the highest number of patients with 11,249
- Medicare (11,154)

## Observation 6 

The analysis of test results across medical conditions shows a relatively even distribution between Abnormal, Normal, and Inconclusive outcomes. Arthristis recorded the highest abnormal result count (3,188), while Asthma had the highest normal result count (3,147). Cancer showed 3,118 abnormal, 3,049 normal, and 3,060 inconclusive results, indicating minimal variation across categories. Diabetes recorded 3,168 abnormal results (one of the highest among all conditions) while Hypertension had the highest normal outcomes at 3,142. Obesity also maintained closely distributed values across all result types, with 3,132 abnormal, 3,057 normal, and 3,042 inconclusive results.  

Overall, the data suggest that test outcomes are consistently distributed across the various medical conditions, with no condition showing an extreme deviation in result patterns. 


## Observation 7 

The analysis of hospital admission had a sharp growth from 2019 to 2020 (Covid–19 era), followed by a relatively stable admission trend over 2020 and 2023 with monthly patient admission fluctuating within a consistent range. Although minor increases and decline were recorded across different months and quarters, there is no clear evidence of a strong seasonal pattern in admission rates. Admission volumes remained generally steady, indicating a consistent demand for healthcare services throughout the years. 

Additionally, the sharp decline observed toward the end of 2024, falling by more than half compared to previous years. 


# Recommendation

Based on the analysis of hospital patient data, the following recommendations are proposed to improve hospital operations, patient care, and resource utilization: 

## Balanced Resource Allocation Across Diseases 

Since the top medical conditions (Arthritis, Diabetes, Hypertension, Cancer, Obesity, and Asthma) occur at relatively similar frequencies, hospitals should avoid over-prioritizing a single condition. Instead, resources such as specialist doctors, diagnostic tools, and treatment facilities should be evenly distributed across all major disease areas.  

 
## Strengthen Adult Healthcare Programs 
The majority of cases occur within the adult age group (18–85). Hospitals should expand chronic disease management programs targeting adults, particularly long-term conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and arthritis. 

  
## Gender-Neutral Healthcare Planning 
Since disease distribution is nearly equal between males and females, healthcare strategies should remain gender-inclusive. Screening and awareness programs should target both groups equally without bias.  


## Optimize Hospital Performance Benchmarking 
Hospitals such as Johnson PLC and LLC Smith show higher billing and admission volumes. These facilities can be used as benchmark institutions to identify best practices in patient handling, billing efficiency, and service delivery, which can be replicated in lower-performing hospitals.  


## Improve Admission Type Cost Management 
Because elective, urgent, and emergency admissions show similar billing totals, hospitals should strengthen cost monitoring systems across all admission categories to ensure efficiency and avoid unnecessary expenditure inflation.  


## Enhance Insurance Collaboration and Policy Planning 

Since insurance providers show almost equal patient distribution, hospitals should maintain balanced partnerships with insurers. However, slight differences in patient counts suggest room for improved negotiation and service agreements to optimize reimbursement processes.  

 
## Standardize Diagnostic and Test Result Interpretation 
Given the relatively even distribution of abnormal, normal, and inconclusive results, hospitals should ensure consistent diagnostic standards across departments to reduce variability and improve accuracy in test reporting.  

 
## Capacity Planning for Non-Seasonal Demand 
Since admissions show no strong seasonal pattern but a general stability over time, hospitals should adopt year-round capacity planning rather than seasonal staffing models. However, the sharp decline in 2024 should be investigated to determine whether it reflects data quality issues or a real operational change.
